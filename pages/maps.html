MAP ART

An Ode to Maps In the east and west, above and below the equator quiet like pins dropping, and in every black pinprick people keep on living. Mass graves and sudden ruins are out of the picture. Nations' borders are barely visible as if they wavered to be or not. I like maps, because they lie. Because they give no access to the vicious truth. Because great-heartedly, good-naturedly they spread before me a world not of this world. (Translated, from the Polish, by Clare Cavanagh.)
